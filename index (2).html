Inroduction: New Zealand is located in the South Pacific Ocean, New Zealand is an island republic in the South Pacific Ocean, the southwesternmost section of Polynesia. New Zealand is a remote land—one of the last large territories fit for habitation to be colonized and settled—more than 1,000 miles (1,600 kilometers) southeast of Australia, its nearest neighbor

Geography: Mostly mountainous or steep hills, volcanic peaks in the central North Island, and fiords in the far southwest. New Zealand is approximately 1,000 miles long and 280 miles wide at its broadest point. It has a smaller surface area than Colorado but a little larger than the United Kingdom. New Zealand consists of two main islands, the North Island and the South Island, as well as over 700 smaller islands. The Cook Strait separates the north and south islands. New Zealand features around 50 volcanoes, some of which are still active. New Zealand is located at the intersection of the Indo-Australian and Pacific tectonic plates. It lies east of Australia, across the Tasman Sea, and south of the islands of New Caledonia, Fiji, and Tonga.

History and Culture: The first immigrants likely arrived from Polynesia between 1200 and 1300 AD. The islands of New Zealand were the last significant livable land that people colonized. New Zealand’s cultural influences are mostly European and Maori.

Popular Tourist Attractions:

Milford Sound is one of the most famous tourist attractions in New Zealand for its coastal scenery with its dramatic peaks and dark blue waters. Another famous tourist attraction is Queenstown known as the adventure capital, the city offers adrenaline-pumping activities such as bungee jumping & white water rafting.

New Zealand's famous food & cuisine contains

  • Fish & chips- Traditionally served wrapped in newspaper & the fish is usually Tarahiki, hoki, red code, blue warehou, or elephant fish ( elephant shark)- battered fried, and then served with chip
  • Kumara- A starchy root vegetable also known as sweet potato, has been grown in New Zealand for over a thousand years. There are several varieties of kumara including Red kumara (Owairaka) most popular, Gold kumara (Toka Toka) - which has golden skin and a slightly sweeter taste, and Orange Kumara ( Beauregard) - The sweetest variety, with rich orange flesh
  • Whitebait-New Zealand whitebait are the juveniles of five freshwater fish species that migrate upstream from the ocean to locate suitable adult habitats.
  • Rewena bread-The potato starter and fermentation give rēwena bread its unique sweet and sour flavor.
  • Bluff oysters- The oysters, commonly known as "bluffies," are famed for their powerful flavor, which one scribe describes as "saltwater, rocks, seaweed, a meaty sweetness, then a rush of intense brininess, then a long, lingering slightly metallic sweet fresh sea flavor."
  • Meat pie-Meat pie is a hand-sized pie that contains diced or minced meat and gravy, occasionally with onion, mushrooms, or cheese and is commonly eaten as a takeaway food snack. Meat pies. A traditional beef pie with tomato sauce.
  • Paua- Pāua is a huge sea snail with a thick, oval-shaped shell, usually palm-sized. The shell is commonly blue-green or purple, with vibrant colors swirling inside.
